With Viryl machines, your plant will spend more time pressing, and you'll spend less time setting up print runs, cleaning up wasted vinyl, sorting out flawed records and standing by to aid the process, allowing you to keep up with surging demand. Our North American-made presses are easy to use, but if you run into a snag, we’re here for remote support, and your machine is under a year warranty.

The Team

Chad Brown


Chief Executive Officer

Chad’s unique background combines entrepreneurship in the record industry with technical experience in the medical device industry. As a founder of the record label Ultrasound Recordings and former owner and operator of Toronto-based record pressing plant Acme Vinyl, Chad carries first-hand experience of the challenges that have plagued record pressing over the last decade. After closing Acme in 2007, he put his biomedical and software engineering education to use at the Toronto-based medical device start-up Sentinelle Medical, Inc. Chad has a rare vision of both the solutions desperately needed by pressing facilities around the world and what it takes to deliver them.

Rob Brown

P. Eng., BASc.

Chief Operating Officer

Rob brings 15 years of product development, leadership, and customer service experience in the medical device and office equipment industries. He is a licensed Professional Engineer and Mechanical Engineering graduate from the University of Toronto. At medical device company Sentinelle Medical, he supported the company’s trajectory from start-up to aggressive growth, managed product teams and oversaw partnerships with global corporations such as GE Healthcare. Rob gained a strong understanding of business operations, finances and planning while supporting the integration of Sentinelle into Hologic. When that same medical business later divested from Hologic, he led the team responsible for incorporating the operation into a division of Philips Healthcare.

James Hashmi

BASc.

Chief Technology Officer

James has honed his entrepreneurial spirit and versatility by spending nine years in product development roles with fast-paced, innovative companies, including Research In Motion/BlackBerry, Sentinelle Medical and Automation Tooling Systems. His career spans the medical device, advanced automated machinery, automotive multi-stage tool and die, and mobile handset industries. Backed by his professional experience and a University of Waterloo degree in Mechanical Engineering with a specialization in mechatronics, he knows what it takes to commercialize new products in any market. He played a key role in strengthening the international business and technical relationships between Sentinelle Medical and all worldwide entities of Toshiba Medical Systems. James is currently enrolled in a songwriting program at Berklee College of Music Online.

Michael Wybenga

MASc.

Director of Hardware Engineering

Michael’s passion lies in the design and production of dynamic mechanisms and machinery, and his education and career reflect that dedication. He holds a bachelor’s degree in Mechanical Engineering and a master’s degree in Systems Design Engineering from the University of Waterloo. Dietrich Schoenemann


Client Manager

Dietrich has been a professional musician, sound designer and audio engineer for almost two decades. From playing gigs as a teenager in the early 90s to DJing at international electronic dance music events, releasing recordings on both vinyl and CD formats, and doing remixing work and audio mastering, his track record is diverse. He owns and operates the company Complete Mastering, which provides services heavily geared towards vinyl records with lacquer cutting services in-house. Complete Mastering is also home to the vinyl-based record label Complete Distribution. His decades in the industry and strong relationships with pressing plants around the globe give him an insider perspective on the vinyl record pressing business.
